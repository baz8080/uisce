What this measures: supply disruptions announced by Uisce Éireann and how long they took to resolve — not measured availability at anyone's tap. Each notice pin is assumed to affect the Census 2022 Small Areas whose centres lie within 500 m (CSO SAPS populations); the headline percentage is the population-weighted share of person-time not covered by an announced supply disruption. Only hard disruptions count against it: bursts, reservoir / treatment-plant / pump-station interruptions, pump failures, power outages, and unplanned repair works. Investigations, leak detection, hydrant works and installations are shown as works but never count; restrictions, low pressure and discolouration are shown but never count. Median time to fix covers resolved events with a known end. Grades: A ≥ 99.9%, B ≥ 99.75%, C ≥ 99.45%, D ≥ 99.0%, else F; an active boil-water / do-not-drink / do-not-consume notice knocks one grade (discolouration does not). County rankings are robust to the 500 m assumption; the letter grades are calibrated to it. End times are LLM-inferred from notice text; start times are when the notice was published, so durations are a floor on true length. Closed cases with no usable end signal still mark their start day and event counts, but add no disruption time. Full methodology: notes/statuspage-methodology.md.
